01 Blitzllama

AI & ML Infra

Built the full AI & ML stack powering Blitzllama, an AI user insights platform.

Topic Modeling

Clusters feedback data and identifies actionable themes.

High Effort

Built a context-aware clustering system that groups feedback into granular, actionable themes based on underlying intent, not just shared keywords. The model uses deep semantic embeddings plus survey and company context to distinguish similar language with different meanings, such as “notifications are too much” versus “notifications need rich media.” Each theme includes a clear topic label, intent classification, sentiment, and representative quotes so teams can act on it without extra analysis. The system runs unsupervised, works in cold-start settings, and keeps themes stable and interpretable as new feedback comes in.

Trend and Anomaly Detection

Detects statistically significant feedback trends, spikes, and anomalies.

High Effort

Built an anomaly and trend detection system that continuously monitors feedback data to flag statistically significant shifts, both steady upward trends and sudden spikes. The model accounts for seasonality, baseline variance, and natural noise to reduce false alarms. It surfaces emerging issues early, such as a new onboarding bug driving a rise in “login failure” feedback, and highlights sustained increases that point to deeper product friction.

Pattern Identification

Identifies patterns between response data and user attributes.

High Effort

Built an advanced analytics layer that surfaces hidden relationships across survey questions, user attributes, and feedback themes. Enabled cross-question analysis to reveal how responses interact - for example, users giving 5-star satisfaction ratings still flagging friction in specific areas. Implemented correlation analysis to uncover statistically meaningful links between segments (e.g., location, behavior) and specific feedback patterns. Designed to automatically highlight emerging patterns with strong signal (using thresholds and lift metrics), helping teams identify root causes of satisfaction or dissatisfaction faster than manual analysis.

Automated Reports

Generates structured reports with prioritised insights.

Medium Effort

Built an automated reporting system that acted as a virtual product analyst - summarizing feedback, surfacing top revenue opportunities, and highlighting recurring pain points without manual effort. Shipped 10 pre-built report templates covering daily, weekly, and monthly cadences, plus specialized templates like top revenue opportunities and high-LTV user pain points. Each report included drill-down capabilities with filters for date ranges, user cohorts, attributes, and channels. Added AI-driven recommendations that generated hypotheses to validate and suggested next steps. Reports delivered directly to email or Slack, with one-click actions to view related feedback or launch targeted follow-up surveys.

Interview Copilot

Real-time transcription and next question recommendations to level up user interviews.

High Effort

The AI copilot listens to interview audio from any tool, transcribes it in real time, and suggests strong follow-up questions based on what the participant just said. The goal is to help teams run better interviews, especially PMs and founders without formal research training. It reduces the gap between hearing something interesting and asking the right next question. Under the hood, I use speech-to-text with diarization and translation, then LLM workflows that generate targeted probes and clarifying questions on the fly. The result is more consistent interviews and better insights without a dedicated researcher on every call. Was in staging environment only.

Search & Chat

Get grounded analysis and answers to natural text queries.

High Effort

Built a semantic search and AI chat system that lets teams query user feedback in natural language (e.g., “What are enterprise customers frustrated about in onboarding?”) and get grounded, source-linked answers. Powered by embeddings and vector retrieval, the system surfaces the most relevant feedback snippets based on meaning—not just keyword matches. Designed with RAG architecture so responses are generated strictly from retrieved customer data, reducing hallucinations and improving trust. Optimized retrieval quality through semantic chunking, metadata filtering (cohort, date, channel), and prompt design for accurate, context-aware synthesis.

Semantic Chunking

Chunks long texts based on similar meaning rather than hard constraints.

Medium Effort

Built a semantic chunking pipeline that segments long-form feedback and transcripts based on meaning rather than arbitrary character or sentence limits. Using embedding similarity, the system detects natural topic boundaries so each chunk contains a coherent unit of thought. This prevents fragmented context (e.g., splitting a complaint mid-explanation) and improves downstream clustering, search, and RAG retrieval quality.

Screenshot not available - data algorithm feature.

Intent Classification

Classifies short-form user feedback into intent categories with 95%+ accuracy.

High Effort

I built the text classifier that powered the topic modeling workflow. Started from a BERT fine-tuning approach, referenced multi-label classification work (RoBERTa on go_emotions), and trained on a large dataset we sourced and labeled. Most of the work was getting accuracy above 95% on messy user-generated content: short text, typos, slang, fragments. The classifier ran on every piece of feedback entering the system.
